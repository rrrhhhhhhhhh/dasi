About

The Detroit Architecture Summer Institute (DASI) is a new annual, four-day program bringing together a small, selective cohort of students, researchers, and practitioners to engage the intersections of automotive systems, labor histories, and creative infrastructures in Detroit.

The inaugural session will take place June 25–28, 2026, at Wayne State University. Structured as an intensive program of seminars, site visits, and archival work, the institute uses Detroit as both subject and site—foregrounding the material and social conditions that shape the built environment.

Participants will work directly with one of the most significant labor archive collections in the United States, offering close engagement with primary materials that document the histories and ongoing transformations of work, industry, and culture.
