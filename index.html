About

The Detroit Architecture Summer Institute (DASI) is a new annual, four-day program bringing together a small, selective cohort of students, researchers, and practitioners.

Structured as an intensive series of seminars, site visits, and archival work, the institute uses Detroit as both subject and site—foregrounding the material and social conditions that shape the built environment.

Participants will work directly with one of the most significant labor archive collections in the United States.
